# !thesis Evidence Judgment Support

This is command-local support for !thesis. It does not replace GLOBAL.md, SOURCES.md, METRICS.md, OUTPUT.md, ARTIFACTS.md, CLASSIFICATIONS.md, SCORING.md, or RERATING.md.

## Purpose

Use this reference to keep thesis construction falsifiable, evidence-backed, and clearly separated from story, promotion, or unsupported market narrative.

## Thesis pillars

Good thesis pillars should state:

- what must be true,
- what current evidence supports it,
- what remains unproven,
- what would strengthen or weaken it,
- what to monitor.

Prefer 3–6 material pillars when useful. Avoid turning pillars into repeated field dumps.

## Evidence separation

Separate these layers clearly:

- reported facts,
- management claims,
- Midas interpretation,
- future assumptions,
- unproven requirements.

Current evidence should not be blended with future assumptions. A plausible story can be researchable without being proven.

## Weak-evidence and story-stock discipline

When a company has a compelling secular story but weak filing evidence:

- convert the story into falsifiable requirements,
- state what reported evidence is missing,
- avoid treating management TAM, targets, or goals as facts,
- avoid social or promotional sources as thesis proof,
- avoid claiming the market is wrong without evidence,
- identify what future disclosures must show.

Do not preserve `Evidence-Incomplete Thesis` as an active label unless a future approved patch adds it to allowed thesis-status labels. Use plain English instead: current filing evidence may be insufficient to support the thesis yet.

## Disconfirming evidence and thesis breakers

A useful thesis names what could break it.

Include disconfirming evidence when it is material, such as:

- revenue or customer concentration that undermines durability,
- margin or cash-flow weakness that contradicts operating leverage assumptions,
- dilution or financing dependence that weakens per-share value creation,
- churn, demand, pricing, cyclicality, or competitive facts that challenge the growth engine,
- disclosed legal, regulatory, accounting, liquidity, or going-concern issues.

## High-multiple discipline

High-multiple or post-rerate situations require stricter evidence discipline, not automatic valuation conclusions.

- Market context may show expectations are high, but it does not prove the thesis is wrong or right.
- Negative earnings or negative FCF can make earnings and FCF multiples not meaningful; label that instead of forcing a conclusion.
- Do not use cheap, expensive, or fair-value framing by default.
- Focus on what future evidence must prove: revenue durability, margin improvement, cash generation, concentration reduction, lower burn, lower dilution, or execution milestones.

## Local labels and global classifications

- Thesis status labels, if used, are local workflow labels only.
- Update direction and pillar labels are local update workflow labels only.
- They are not Setup Classifications.
- CLASSIFICATIONS.md and SCORING.md remain the authority for setup classifications and scoring.
- Do not create a local classification system or score formula inside `!thesis` references.
