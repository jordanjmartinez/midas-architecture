# !thesis Market and Rerating Support

This is command-local support for !thesis. It does not replace GLOBAL.md, SOURCES.md, METRICS.md, OUTPUT.md, ARTIFACTS.md, CLASSIFICATIONS.md, SCORING.md, or RERATING.md.

## Purpose

Use this reference when market, valuation, expectations, rerating, post-rerate, or overextension context is relevant to a thesis.

## Default boundary

Market data, valuation context, and rerating context are not required by default.

Do not make market data or valuation context a default `!thesis` section.

Use market / valuation / rerating context only when:

- the user explicitly requests it, or
- it is materially needed for thesis framing, rerating-stage context, valuation setup, post-rerate discipline, or a thesis-breaker / catalyst discussion.

## Evidence boundary

Market data can inform expectations context. It does not prove thesis pillars.

Do not use market data, price action, market cap, valuation multiples, volume, or liquidity to prove:

- business quality,
- moat,
- growth engine,
- margin expansion,
- cash-flow durability,
- risk reduction,
- customer claims,
- segment claims,
- management execution.

Do not claim mispricing without evidence. Do not use valuation language as recommendation framing.

## When market data is used

- Label provider/source and as-of date.
- Note timing mismatch between market data and filing-derived fundamentals when relevant.
- Use direct helper/provider data when needed and available.
- Do not parse user-facing `!market` output as a data source.
- If data is stale, incomplete, unavailable, or not reliably labelable, say so and do not guess.

## Metrics and rerating authority

- METRICS.md owns formulas, metric labels, GAAP/non-GAAP discipline, valuation multiple definitions, and calculation quality.
- RERATING.md owns rerating, post-rerate, overextension, market-expectations, and setup-context discipline.
- This reference should not define local valuation formulas or a local scoring framework.

## Story-stock and high-multiple caution

For high-expectation or story-driven companies:

- treat market context as context only,
- convert the story into falsifiable operating and financial evidence requirements,
- avoid cheap / expensive / fair-value default conclusions,
- avoid price targets,
- avoid turning `!thesis` into a full valuation model,
- state when earnings or FCF multiples are not meaningful because earnings or FCF are negative.

The thesis should remain filing-backed even when market expectations matter.
