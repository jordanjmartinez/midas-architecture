# !research Sector Edge-Case Checklist

This is command-local support for `!research`. It does not replace GLOBAL.md, SOURCES.md, OUTPUT.md, METRICS.md, or ARTIFACTS.md.

Use this as a compact reminder for business models where filings can be easy to overinterpret.

## Consumer beauty / consumer brands

- Separate repeat consumption from contractual recurrence; consumables are not automatically recurring revenue.
- Check retailer, marketplace, distributor, and wholesale concentration.
- Distinguish brand heat, product launches, shelf space, replenishment, and DTC engagement from locked-in demand.
- Watch sourcing, China/tariff exposure, third-party manufacturers, logistics, product-safety/regulatory risk, and recall exposure.
- Separate acquired-brand contribution from organic or existing-business growth when filings disclose it.
- Watch marketing intensity and whether efficiency metrics are disclosed.

## Freemium / subscription / app models

- Separate subscription revenue from bookings, billings, deferred revenue, ads, in-app purchases, tests/certifications, and other revenue.
- Deferred revenue often reflects prepaid consumer subscriptions; do not treat it as long-term enterprise lock-in without support.
- Track users, paid subscribers/users, conversion, retention, churn, DAU/MAU, and engagement only when disclosed.
- Treat app-store, payment-processor, and platform dependence as distribution/payment concentration, not necessarily customer concentration.
- Consumer subscriptions may have recurring characteristics but are often easy to cancel and discretionary.

## Fabless / power semiconductor transition stories

- Do not infer AI, data-center, EV, grid, or industrial revenue from target-market language alone.
- Identify actual disclosed product, segment, end-market, geography, customer, and channel mix; say when not disclosed.
- Design wins, pipeline, engagements, and platform partnerships are not revenue and are not recurring revenue.
- Backlog should be checked for cancellation, reduction, delay, and concentration terms.
- Distributor/customer concentration, receivables concentration, inventory write-downs, supplier exits, and channel resets can break the model.
- Fabless supply transitions, foundry/wafer/packaging/test dependencies, yield issues, and requalification delays can be thesis-critical.
